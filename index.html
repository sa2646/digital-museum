Carnival Through Time

1700s

Early Carnival Traditions

Portuguese colonists brought entrudo festivals, featuring street celebrations with water, flour, and eggs.

1900s

Rise of Samba Schools

First samba schools formed in Rio's neighborhoods, creating organized parades with themes and costumes.

1984

Sambadrome Construction

Architect Oscar Niemeyer designed the permanent parade ground, professionalizing the competition.

Today

Global Cultural Celebration

Carnival attracts millions worldwide, showcasing Brazilian culture while preserving community traditions.

Parade Float Design

Catalog #CM.2024.001

Spectacular parade floats featuring intricate bird motifs represent the freedom and joy of Carnival. These moving sculptures transform the Sambadrome into a living gallery of Brazilian artistry.

Origin: Rio de Janeiro, 2024
Materials: Steel framework, fabric, feathers, paint
Cultural Significance: Symbol of artistic freedom and community creativity

Samba Performance

Catalog #CM.2024.002

The heart of Carnival lies in these powerful performances where dancers become living embodiments of rhythm and movement. Each samba school spends months preparing their competitive presentation.

Origin: Sambadrome, Rio de Janeiro
Tradition: Annual competition since 1932
Cultural Significance: Preservation of Afro-Brazilian heritage

Community Celebration

Catalog #CM.2024.003

Millions gather to witness and participate in Carnival, creating a collective experience of joy and cultural affirmation. The crowd itself becomes part of the artistic expression.

Participants: 2+ million annually
Duration: 5 days of celebration
Cultural Significance: Democratic celebration of Brazilian identity

The Sambadrome

Catalog #CM.2024.004

This iconic stadium serves as the sacred space where Carnival's competitive performances unfold. Designed by Oscar Niemeyer, it represents modern Brazilian architecture meeting traditional celebration.

Architect: Oscar Niemeyer
Capacity: 90,000 spectators
Cultural Significance: Modern temple of Carnival tradition
